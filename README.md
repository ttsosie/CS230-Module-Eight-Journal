# CS230-Module-Eight-Journal
This is a submission of my portfolio and its artifacts. This will include the design document I submitted for module seven, The Gaming Room. 

# Briefly summarize The Gaming Room client and their software requirements.
The client for this project was The Gaming Room, a company that currently offered Draw It or Lose It as an Android application. They wanted to expand the game into a web-based application that could support multiple operating systems instead of being limited to Android devices. The software needed to support multiple teams with multiple players, maintain unique game, team, and player names, and ensure only one instance of a game existed at a time through the Singleton design pattern. The client also wanted a solution that was scalable, secure, and capable of operating in a distributed environment.

# What did you do particularly well in developing this documentation?
I think one of my strengths was organizing my recommendations by explaining not only what I recommended but also why I recommended it. Throughout the project I compared Linux, Windows, macOS, and mobile platforms before making a recommendation instead of selecting an operating system without justification. Breaking each recommendation into several supporting reasons helped me better understand the tradeoffs between different operating systems and made my documentation easier to follow.

# What about the process of working through a design document did you find helpful when developing the code?
Creating the design document before writing code helped me understand the application's architecture and requirements before implementation. The UML diagram, domain model, and evaluation of operating systems allowed me to understand how the different classes related to one another and why patterns such as Singleton and Iterator were appropriate for this project. By thinking through the design first, implementing the Java code became much more straightforward because I already understood how the application should function.

# If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?
If I were to revise one section, I would improve my early platform evaluations. At the beginning of the project, I focused mostly on comparing operating systems. After completing the course, I have a much better understanding of cloud computing, distributed systems, storage management, memory management, and security. I would incorporate more discussion about cloud-based architectures and explain more clearly how those concepts support scalability and long-term software maintenance.

# How did you interpret the user's needs and implement them into your software design? Why is it so important to consider the user's needs when designing?
I interpreted the client's needs by identifying both the business requirements and the technical requirements before making design decisions. Rather than choosing technologies simply because they were popular, I considered the client's goals of expanding beyond Android, supporting multiple platforms, keeping costs reasonable, and maintaining a secure and scalable application. Understanding the user's needs is important because every design decision affects the usability, performance, cost, and future growth of the software. A design that ignores the client's requirements may work technically but fail to solve the actual business problem.

# How did you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a similar software application?
Throughout this project I learned that software design should begin with understanding the problem before writing code. I approached the project by identifying the requirements, comparing different operating platforms, evaluating their advantages and disadvantages, and then selecting the solution that best met the client's needs. In the future, I would continue using design documents, UML diagrams, architectural comparisons, and requirement analysis before development begins. I also learned the importance of considering scalability, security, and maintainability early in the design process because those decisions become much more difficult to change after development has started.
